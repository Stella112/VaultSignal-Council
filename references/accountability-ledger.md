# Agent Accountability Ledger

Use this reference when creating proof for judges or reviewing live performance.

## Purpose

The ledger records the six-agent council's assumptions before a trade and reviews those assumptions after an exit, watchlist expiry, or block decision. It is designed to show discipline, not to claim guaranteed profit.

## Entry Ledger

```text
Agent Accountability Ledger
- Alpha Intake: <claim> | Evidence: <source/freshness/token> | Review: <trigger>
- Smart Money: <claim> | Evidence: <wallet count/sold ratio> | Review: <trigger>
- Shield: <claim> | Evidence: <risk level/security result> | Review: <trigger>
- Market Structure: <claim> | Evidence: <liquidity/holders/volume> | Review: <trigger>
- ExitSense: <claim> | Evidence: <entry quote/exit quote/impact> | Review: <trigger>
- Vault Risk: <claim> | Evidence: <balance/position/drawdown> | Review: <trigger>
```

## Post-Trade Review

```text
Agent Accountability Review
Outcome: <profit|loss|watchlist expired|blocked correctly>
Best Agent: <agent and reason>
Weakest Assumption: <agent and reason>
Rule Adjustment: <one small future change or none>
```

## Judge Value

- Shows the skill is not a blind swapper.
- Makes blocked trades useful evidence.
- Creates a repeatable review loop for live performance.
- Keeps the strategy understandable without adding complex math.
